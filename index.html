Client Profile: QuickEats

This section details the client's core objectives for this POC and identifies the key teams we will be working with.

Primary Objectives

  • 1. Seamless Migration

    They need a smooth transition from their current provider with minimal data loss or disruption to their marketing operations.

  • 2. Fraud Protection

    Validating the effectiveness of Protect360 is critical. This is likely driven by their expansion into LATAM, where mobile ad fraud rates can be high.

  • 3. Granular Data Export

    The requirement to export data to BigQuery points to a data-driven team. They rely on granular insights for their internal business intelligence and modeling.

  • 4. Re-engagement & Deep Linking

    They want to leverage our advanced tools to increase customer lifetime value (LTV) by effectively re-engaging their existing user base.

Key Stakeholder Groups

Marketing/UA Team

These are the primary business owners of the POC. Their success is measured by campaign performance and re-engagement KPIs. They will be our main point of contact.

Data/BI Team

This team owns the BigQuery integration. They will be laser-focused on data accuracy, granularity, and the timeliness of the data we provide. We must earn their trust.

Engineering/Product Team

Responsible for the technical heavy lifting: implementing the SDK and building the in-app routing logic for deep linking. Clear guidance and support are crucial for them.

Anticipated Risks & Mitigation Plan

This section outlines potential technical challenges and our proactive strategies to ensure a smooth POC. Click on each risk to see our mitigation plan.

Mitigation: Provide clear documentation on best practices for parallel SDK implementation. Set up a dedicated support channel with their engineers to troubleshoot any performance flags immediately. Carefully manage which SDK initializes first if needed.

Mitigation: Proactively set expectations during the kick-off call. Prepare a "source of truth" document explaining the key differences in our attribution models (e.g., lookback windows, fingerprinting logic). Frame our data as the more accurate, reliable source.

Mitigation: Schedule a dedicated Deep Linking workshop early in the process (Phase 4). Provide them with code samples and a step-by-step guide. Start with a simple use case to demonstrate value quickly before tackling more complex scenarios.

Mitigation: Engage their Data/BI team early to understand their existing schema. Review our Data Locker and BigQuery connector documentation with them, highlighting customization options. Be prepared to assist with mapping our data fields to their internal structure.

Our Winning Message

These are the two key value messages we must emphasize throughout the POC to align with the client's core business objectives.

"Your Single Source of Truth for Growth."

This message directly addresses their need for reliable, granular data in BigQuery. We must emphasize that accurate attribution isn't just a technical check-box; it's the foundation of their entire marketing intelligence stack. Our robust raw-data reporting and reliable BigQuery connector give their data team the trustworthy foundation they need to make critical business decisions.

"Turn Installs into Loyal Customers."

This shifts the conversation from deep linking as a technical feature to a core driver of business growth. For a competitive food delivery app, re-engaging a user for a second or third order is paramount. We will frame our re-engagement suite (OneLink, Audiences) as the most effective toolset to increase LTV and win in the hyper-competitive LATAM market.
